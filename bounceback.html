Overview

Bounce Back was my 2nd master’s semester project. For this project, I took part in creating a physical system intended to aid people in taking frequent micro-breaks from their work, as studies have shown this to increase productivity and quality of work. The project took an unorthodox approach to this by studying people who smoked, to understand the practice of taking frequent social smoking breaks. The aim of this was to create a system that emulated the core values of this practice, but without the smoking aspect.
My primary roles were: Concept creation, user research, as well as the design, 3D-modelling, and creation of the physical device.

Process

For this project, I conducted interviews with regular smokers. I then analysed the data using a top-down approach, aimed at identifying the competences, materials, and meanings associated with the practice of smoking. These then served as requirements for the system.
Through an iterative process of ideation and design, we landed on the concept of Bounce Back, which is a physical system consisting of dock and a ball. When the ball is removed from the dock, all other Bounce Back systems in the network, typically belonging to one’s immediate co-workers, would then indicate that a person is intending to take a short break in 1 minute. By removing their ball from the dock, they can then signal to others that they are joining the break.
I designed and developed the dock for the system. This was done through an iterative process of creating physical prototypes in paper and modelling foam, before creating the final design using the CAD software SolidWorks, which was then 3D-printed.
