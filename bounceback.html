Study Space Spotter

Case & Product

Study space spotter is an interactive map which shows the location, characteristics, and availability of study spaces at Aalborg University Library. For this project my study group and I worked directly with the library to solve a specific problem they experienced. The map allows a user to filter study spaces based on how many people it sits, its available equipment, whether its formal or informal (i.e sofas and lounge chairs vs. office chairs and a table), and whether or not it’s located in a quiet zone of the library. Additionally, the map provides a route to the study space. As a primary concern was the speed at which it could be used, the map is designed to all fit on one screen, and the study space indicators are made to show key characteristics at a glance, making the filtering option ignorable for people who regularly uses the library and know where they like to sit. Additionally, it functions as an ordinary map, showing the location of toilets, printers, and book collections, etc.

Process

The project took place over three distinct phases. For phase 1 we met with a representative of the library to understand the details of the problem, after which we created a catalogue of broad ideas from which the library representative picked one – A way for students to tell which study spaces were occupied. Phase 2 consisted of ideating on the chosen idea, the idea of individual sensors for each study space, and the filtering option was added during this phase. Lastly, for phase 3, a total of 4 usability tests were conducted. For these tests I took the role of facilitator, guiding the participants through a total of 9 scenarios, prompting them when they got stuck, and inquiring about their choices. After each test I conducted an interview with the participant. Based on the findings of these tests, the initial requirements were reevaluated, and a final version of the product was designed.
