Master Thesis Project

For my master thesis project I designed an app intended to help people reduce their electricity spending through helping shift their electricity usage.
The project was completed in collaboration with NRGi, and was based on a participatory design process.

Pre-project

Before the actual master thesis project, I conducted a pre-project focused on on user research.
First, I conducted a group interview focused on how people currently felt about their electricity usage, the ways in which they controlled it, and their challenges and motivations associated with this.

I then created three simple prototype-concepts, which were presented to a group of people to discuss and build upon.


Deployment

I then designed a prototype version of the app, and (through some Figma trickery) created a functional version fit for a deployment test.

Using this app, I conducted a deployment test with six participants across four households. Each participant was asked to use the app for one week, and I conducted interviews with each participant before and after the deployment.

The post-deployment interviews were analyzed in a top-down manner using a practice framework created by Shove et al as the categorization framework.


For this, I first coded all of the interviews, creating codes related to desires, challenges, ideas, etc.
I then clustered all similar ideas, before categorizing them in the framework.
Lastly, i drew connections between clusters that related to one another, or were interdependent.
From this, a total of 13 interconnected elements were uncovered. These elements and their connections functioned as requirements for the final design.

Final Design

Lastly, I created the final prototype through a typical iterative design process, using the aforementioned requirements to justify and create features.
