Pantree

Case & My Role

For my first semester project as part of my master’s, I took part in creating the digital system Pantree. For this project my primary roles were: Planning, conducting, and analysing a group interview; concept-creation, and prototyping.
Pantree is an app intended to run on a shared tablet in a collective household in which multiple people co-habitate in such a way that there is a mix of personal and shared food items. The system intends to aid the inhabitants of the collective household in reducing food waste through providing an overview of all food items and their estimated expiry date, as well as tools to indicate which items are free to take, and suggestions for how to use food based on who is dining together and what is in their shared personal food storage.

Process

Pantree was created following a traditional human-centered design approach consisting of 3 steps: Research, ideation, and prototyping.
For the research portion, we conducted a workshop with four members of a larger shared household. This workshop consisted of a group interview and an inspiration card ideation session, both of which I planned and conducted. We analysed the results of this workshop using an inductive thematic analysis.
Based on the findings of the analysis, we established a set of core values, needs, and pain points related to managing a large ever-changing stock of private and shared food. With these serving as guidings marks, we held an ideation session following the 6-3-5 ideation method – The result of which was the concept of Pantree.
The final part of the process was entirely standard, multiple designs were sketched and wireframed in an iterative fashion, before creating a prototype of the final product using Figma.
Had the project continued, the next step would have been to conduct user testing with the participating household members.
